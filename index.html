Stolac, Bosnia & Herzegovina

Visit Stolac

Located in the Herzegovina-Neretva Canton of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Stolac is a captivating town and municipality nestled in the picturesque region of Herzegovina. Positioned along the tourist route that traverses Herzegovina, it serves as a vital link connecting the mountainous Bosnian hinterland with the coastal regions of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Dubrovnik, and Montenegro. Situated in the Herzegovina Humina area, Stolac benefits from a strategic location on a route that spans from Sarajevo through Mostar, Stolac, Ljubinje, and Trebinje, facilitating a journey to Dubrovnik in under 4 hours. The town's favorable natural environment, including its geological composition, contours, climate, hydrography, and vegetation, has attracted settlers since ancient times. Thanks to its rich hunting grounds and other natural advantages, Stolac and its surroundings have been inhabited by prehistoric communities, as well as by the Illyrians, Romans, and Slavs, all of whom have left behind a wealth of anthropological evidence
